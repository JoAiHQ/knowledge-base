# What is an AI Agent?

You've heard the term everywhere. But what *actually* is an AI agent?

**An AI agent is software that can think, decide, and act on its own to complete a goal.**

That's it. No PhD required.

## The Simple Difference

Here's the easiest way to understand it:

| Regular AI | AI Agent |
|------------|----------|
| You ask, it answers | You give a goal, it figures out how |
| One response, done | Multiple steps, adapts as needed |
| Needs your constant input | Works independently |

**Example:**

- **Regular AI:** "Write me a meeting invite email."
- **AI Agent:** "Schedule a meeting with Sarah next week." → Checks your calendar, finds a free slot, checks Sarah's calendar, sends the invite, adds a reminder.

Same starting point. Wildly different outcomes.

## The Three Superpowers of an AI Agent

Every true AI agent has three core abilities:

### 1. Perceive

An agent can "see" information from outside itself. This might be:
- Your emails
- Your calendar
- A database
- The internet

Without perception, it's just guessing in the dark.

### 2. Reason

This is where the "intelligence" comes in. The agent thinks:
- *What is my goal?*
- *What information do I have?*
- *What should I do first?*

It breaks big problems into smaller steps and figures out the right order.

### 3. Act

This is what separates agents from chatbots. Agents have **tools**—the ability to *do* things:
- Send an email
- Create a document
- Update a spreadsheet
- Book a flight

An agent without action is just a very smart rock.

## A Real-World Analogy

Think of it like hiring an assistant.

**A chatbot** is like texting a friend who knows a lot. They'll answer your questions, but they can't *do* anything for you.

**An AI agent** is like hiring an assistant who can access your apps, check your calendar, and take action on your behalf. You say "handle my inbox," and they actually handle it.

## Why Should You Care?

Because agents don't just save time. They give you **leverage**.

The average knowledge worker spends [28% of their day](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/the-social-economy) on email alone. Add meetings, scheduling, and data entry—and suddenly most of your day is maintenance, not meaningful work.

AI agents can handle that maintenance automatically:
- Triage your inbox
- Schedule your meetings
- Update your CRM after calls
- Create reports from raw data
- **Even pay invoices on your behalf**

Imagine this: an invoice lands in your inbox. Your agent reads it, verifies it matches the expected amount from your contract, and pays it directly from your wallet—no bank logins, no manual transfers.

That's not science fiction. Agents with access to digital wallets can execute payments autonomously. You set the rules ("only pay approved vendors under $500"), and the agent handles the rest.

You focus on the work only *you* can do.
